Military Integrated
Tourism Center

Project overview

This project involves the comprehensive design and development of a multi-functional campus dedicated to military heritage and veteran welfare. By integrating high-traffic public spaces—such as a state-of-the-art auditorium, sports complex, and interactive military museum—the center serves as a bridge between the armed forces and the civilian community.

Details

My contributions have been instrumental in orchestrating the complex spatial planning required to balance public tourism with secure military administration. I oversaw every aspect of the project's lifecycle, from architectural conceptualization to the construction administration of specialized medical and retail facilities.

Area of site 14188 m²

Tools used Autocad, SketchUp

Results

The Military Integrated Tourism Center evolved from an academic thesis into a formally proposed urban development project in collaboration with Aayojan Architects. The project's selection for professional proposal was driven by its innovative approach to "Integrated Welfare," successfully mapping the complex requirements of a Zila Sainik Kariyalaya, ECHS Polyclinic, and CSD Canteen into a unified, high-security campus.

The design achieved a unique balance between rigorous military administration and public engagement, featuring a landmark Shaheed Smarak and Military Museum designed to serve as a regional tourism anchor. This project stands as a successful case study in architectural diplomacy, demonstrating how commemorative heritage and essential veteran services can be synthesized into a modern, sustainable urban fabric.
