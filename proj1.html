Military Integrated
Tourism Center

Project overview

This project involves the comprehensive design and development of a multi-functional campus dedicated to military heritage and veteran welfare. By integrating high-traffic public spaces-such as a state-of-the-art auditorium, sports complex, and interactive military museum-with essential service hubs like the Zila Sainik Kariyalaya, ECHS polyclinic, and CSD canteen, Skill Development Center, the center serves as a bridge between the armed forces and the civilian community. The goal is to create a sustainable, accessible landmark that honors the legacy of our martyrs at the Shaheed Smarak while providing modernized infrastructure for the defense family.

Details

My contributions have been instrumental in orchestrating the complex spatial planning required to balance public tourism with secure military administration. I oversaw every aspect of the project's lifecycle, from the architectural conceptualization of the commemorative zones to the construction administration of the specialized medical and retail facilities. This included ensuring that the aesthetic dignity of the museum and memorial was maintained while delivering the functional rigor necessary for the sports complex and administrative offices.

Results

The Military Integrated Tourism Center evolved from an academic thesis into a formally proposed urban development project in collaboration with Aayojan Architects. The project's selection for professional proposal was driven by its innovative approach to "Integrated Welfare, successfully mapping the complex requirements of a Zila Sainik Kariyalaya, ECHS Polyclinic, and CSD Canteen into a unified, high-security campus. The design achieved a unique balance between rigorous military administration and public engagement, featuring a landmark Shaheed Smarak and Military Museum designed to serve as a regional tourism anchor. This project stands as a successful case study in architectural diplomacy, demonstrating how commemorative heritage and essential veteran services can be synthesized into a modern, sustainable urban fabric.
